RESEARCH

Research Projects

Work spanning stormwater hydrology, compound flood risk, machine learning, and climate & energy policy across the Gulf Coast and South Asia.

Green Infrastructure Monitoring & PCSWMM Modeling

Kreole Elementary School, Moss Point, MS

Field-calibrated PCSWMM model of a bioswale and rain garden system, built on a corrected HOBO water-level dataset (two-step pressure-correction workflow in R) and evaluated against NOAA Atlas 14 design storms. Core chapter of current PhD thesis.

Compound Flood Driver Analysis

Moss Point / Pascagoula River Basin, MS · 2008–2024

Classified bivariate and multivariate compound flood events by combining daily rainfall, USGS river discharge, and NOAA tide-gauge non-tidal residuals. Includes cross-correlation lag analysis and updated IDF curves using AMS/GEV methods.

Machine Learning for Flood Resilience Planning

Moss Point, MS

Random Forest and XGBoost models with Leave-One-Ward-Out spatial cross-validation and SHAP interpretation, paired with MCDA/TOPSIS prioritization of nature-based flood mitigation and bootstrap uncertainty quantification.

Air Conditioning Proliferation & Cooling Policy

Bangladesh

Scenario modeling of national AC demand growth (business-as-usual, moderate, and accelerated efficiency-policy pathways) and its implications for electricity demand and refrigerant emissions.

Decoupling of Rainfall Totals & Extreme Rainfall

Bangladesh · CHIRPS-based

Analysis of how total and extreme rainfall trends are diverging across Bangladesh, with spatial and seasonal breakdowns of the divergence.

Long-Term Aerosol Optical Depth Trends

Bangladesh · 25-year MODIS Terra record

Trend and change-point analysis of atmospheric aerosol optical depth using Mann-Kendall, Sen's slope, and PELT methods, built on Google Earth Engine workflows.

Post-Disaster Forest Recovery

Rohingya Refugee Region, Bangladesh

NDVI-based remote sensing analysis of vegetation recovery following large-scale displacement and land-use change, developed using Google Earth Engine.
